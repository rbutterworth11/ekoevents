Real Wedding: A Winter Celebration at Highcliffe Castle

8 January 2026 | 6 min read | EKO Wedding Films

When Mr and Mrs Hartley chose Highcliffe Castle for their December wedding, they wanted a celebration that embraced the season rather than fighting it. The result was one of the most visually striking winter weddings we have ever filmed.

The venue

Highcliffe Castle sits on the clifftops overlooking Christchurch Bay. The Grade I listed building combines Gothic Revival architecture with panoramic sea views, and in winter, the dramatic coastal setting takes on a moody, atmospheric quality that is impossible to replicate in summer.

The couple chose the Great Hall for their ceremony. The high ceilings, stained glass windows, and stone walls create a space that feels both grand and intimate. In December, the low afternoon sun streams through the west-facing windows at exactly the right angle during late afternoon ceremonies, flooding the hall with warm, golden light. This natural light is exceptional for filming and produces footage with a richness and depth that artificial lighting cannot match.

The day

Bridal preparations took place at a nearby hotel, where the morning light provided soft, flattering conditions for capturing the details: the dress, the shoes, the quiet moments of anticipation before the day begins.

The ceremony itself was held in the Great Hall at 2pm, timed to catch the afternoon light at its best. The couple wrote their own vows, which we captured with wireless lapel microphones to ensure every word was crystal clear. The acoustic properties of the Great Hall are excellent for audio recording. The stone walls create a warm, resonant quality that gives spoken words a natural depth.

After the ceremony, we used the castle grounds and the coastal path for couple portraits. Winter light in Dorset is low and golden for much of the afternoon, which gave us an extended window for stunning portrait footage. The clifftop setting with the sea behind created cinematic compositions that would work as frames in any feature film.

The reception

The evening reception moved to a marquee on the castle grounds, decorated with candlelight and winter foliage. The transition from the ancient grandeur of the castle to the warm intimacy of the marquee gave the film a natural narrative arc: from anticipation to ceremony to celebration.

The speeches were a highlight. The best man's speech had the room in tears of laughter, while the father of the bride delivered words that brought genuine emotion from every guest. These moments are where wedding videography earns its value. No photograph can capture the timing of a punchline or the way a room collectively holds its breath.

Why winter weddings work beautifully on film

There is a misconception that summer weddings make better films. In our experience, winter weddings often produce the most atmospheric and emotionally powerful footage. The light is warmer and softer. The pace is more relaxed. The shorter days create natural transitions from daylight ceremony to candlelit evening that give the film a cinematic progression.

At Highcliffe Castle, the winter setting added a layer of drama that perfectly matched the Gothic architecture. The moody sky over the sea, the bare trees along the coastal path, the warm glow from the castle windows against the darkening sky: these contrasts create visual storytelling that summer simply cannot deliver.

Videography details

Coverage: 10 hours, from bridal preparations through to the first dance

Team: Husband and wife dual-camera setup

Equipment: Sony A7 cameras, wireless audio, drone (conditions permitted limited aerial footage due to coastal wind)

Deliverables: Full wedding film (52 minutes), highlight film (4 minutes), individual ceremony, speeches, and first dance films. Delivered via private online gallery and USB gift box.
