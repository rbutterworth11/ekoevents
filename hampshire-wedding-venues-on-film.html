10 Hampshire Wedding Venues That Look Incredible on Film

28 November 2025 | 10 min read | EKO Wedding Films

Hampshire offers some of the most diverse wedding venue options in the south of England. From the historic streets of Winchester to the rolling countryside of the Test Valley, from the Solent coast to the ancient woodlands of the New Forest, the county provides backdrops that suit every style of celebration and every taste in wedding film.

As wedding videographers covering Hampshire for over a decade, we have filmed at venues across the county and know which ones consistently produce the most beautiful footage. Here are our ten favourites.

1. Northbrook Park, Farnham

A Georgian country house set within 120 acres of Hampshire parkland. The Vine Room, with its soaring ceiling and floor-to-ceiling windows, provides exceptional natural light for ceremony filming. The parkland offers sweeping views for drone footage and golden hour portraits. The scale of the grounds means there is always a quiet, beautiful spot for intimate couple footage away from guests.

2. Tithe Barn, Hampshire

One of the finest medieval barns in the south of England. The 13th-century timber-framed structure has an architectural grandeur that films beautifully: soaring oak beams, stone walls, and generous proportions that allow natural light to fill the space. Acoustically, the barn captures speeches and vows with a warm resonance. The surrounding gardens and countryside provide contrasting outdoor settings.

3. Marwell Hotel

Set within the grounds of Marwell Zoo, this venue offers something genuinely unique. The combination of elegant hotel interiors and the surrounding wildlife park means wedding films from here have a character unlike anywhere else in Hampshire. The landscaped gardens are beautiful for outdoor ceremonies, and the unexpected wildlife encounters add memorable moments to every film.

4. Rivervale Barn, Yateley

A purpose-built barn venue that combines rustic charm with modern facilities. The oak-framed barn has large windows that flood the space with light, making it one of the easiest venues in Hampshire to film in. The adjoining courtyard garden is perfect for outdoor ceremonies, and the surrounding fields provide a simple, uncluttered backdrop for couple portraits.

5. The Elvetham, Hartley Wintney

A stunning Victorian country house set in 35 acres of grounds. The ornamental lake, formal gardens, and grand architecture provide a wealth of visual settings within the estate. For wedding films, the approach along the tree-lined drive creates a cinematic opening, while the lakeside setting offers reflections and natural beauty for portrait sequences.

6. Froyle Park, Alton

An 18th-century country house with elegant interiors and extensive grounds. The orangery provides a light-filled ceremony space with views across the gardens. The walled garden is ideal for intimate outdoor moments, and the parkland offers open space for aerial drone footage. The venue has excellent natural light throughout, which simplifies filming and produces consistently beautiful results.

7. Clock Barn, Whitchurch

A beautifully converted Hampshire barn surrounded by countryside. The exposed brick and timber interior has a warm, textured quality that films exceptionally well by both natural and candlelight. The pond and willow trees in the grounds provide a romantic outdoor setting for portraits, and the barn doors open onto views of rolling farmland.

8. Southampton Harbour Hotel

For couples who prefer a contemporary, waterfront setting, Southampton Harbour Hotel offers stunning views across Ocean Village Marina. The modern interiors and harbour-side terrace provide a sleek backdrop for wedding films, with boats and water adding movement and visual interest. Evening receptions benefit from the reflected light on the water.

9. Audleys Wood Hotel, Basingstoke

A Victorian country house hotel surrounded by ancient woodland. The conservatory ceremony room floods with natural light, while the woodland grounds provide atmospheric portrait settings. The autumn colour in the surrounding trees makes this a particularly strong venue for weddings between September and November.

10. The Winchester Hotel

Located in the heart of Winchester, this venue offers proximity to the city's historic cathedral, medieval streets, and the South Downs. Couples who want an urban wedding with countryside on the doorstep find this combination compelling. The city provides texture and variety for wedding films, with the ancient architecture of Winchester adding gravitas and history to every frame.
